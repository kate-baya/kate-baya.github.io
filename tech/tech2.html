Margin, Border, and Padding.


Both Margin and Padding refer to the space around elements. The main point of difference is the space that each property targets.

Using the example of sending a package to a friend, I'll attempt to explain the use of Margin, Border, and Padding from a different perspective.

Border

In order to get a clear picture of the different areas targets by Margin and Padding, lets first look at the Border property. The Border property manages the style, width and color of an element's boundary.

    Border Properties
  • Border-width: Determines how thick the border is.
  • Border-style: Determines what kind of line a surrounds the element.
  • Color: Determines what the color of the border will be.

Regardless of whether a border is included in the styling of an element, the boundary of an elament always exists and this will affect the Margin and Padding properties.

Now let's get ready to send that package I talked about earlier! I have some books that I am wanting to post to a friend. First things first, I need a box to place them in. In this example, the books sitting inside the box are the content, and the box surrounding the books is the border.

Padding

Padding targets the space inside the border of an element. You can determine the space of the padding by using lengths or percentages and cannot use negative values. Because padding is invisible, it is useful if you are wanting to move content within a container without altering the state of the container itself.

Going back to the example of the package, in order to prevent the books being damaged in transit, we want to place some protective packing between them and the box/border. The packaging is going to act as padding between the box/border and its contents. This the same within css.

Margin

Margin targets the space outside the border of an element. You can determine the space of the margin by using lengths, percentages or "auto" and can have negative values. It is important to note that Margin will take the parent element's style, while Padding is transparent. If you had a blue background for your content and used padding to move that content, the blue background would not be altered.

The books are secured within the box and we are finally ready to deliver the package. Any space outside of the box can be considered the margin, but for the purposes of this example lets assume the box of books is sitting within a delivery van. The space between the walls, floor, and roof of the van is going to be the margin. No matter where we move the box within the van, the contents of the box remain in place.
