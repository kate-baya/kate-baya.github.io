Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is how we use our emotions to communicate with others. A positive EQ would mean we are using our emotions in a way that is empathetic, stress relieving and defusing way. Emotional EQ can be seen as street smarts, where we are focusing on our interactions and relations with others to achieve our goals.

Difference between EQ and IQ

IQ is different to EQ in that it is measured by tests and scores your ability to think logically and answer questions typically in the form of puzzles. For me, I see IQ as being more about what you know rather than how you behave. EQ on the otherhand is all about our behaviour and how we control our emotions. Someone who has a high IQ will be able to answer most math equations, whereas someone with high EQ and low IQ probably wouldn't be able to. Alternatively, someone with high EQ might be able to better reason with someone standing on the edge of a high building, but someone with low EQ and high IQ might not. Both EQ and IQ are strengths and we each have different levels that contribute to our overall intelligence and personality.

Important of EQ

Our level of EQ and it's associated skills helps us to cope with negative emotions. By using our EQ we can manage our stress, anxiety, and fears. EQ is very helpful for keeping our mental health in check and without it, we would all be lacking in personality. Thanks to EQ we aren't rule function based robots, and we can communicate how we feel to others.
